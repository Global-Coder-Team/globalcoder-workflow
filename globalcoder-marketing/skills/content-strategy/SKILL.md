# Content Strategy

## Overview

Map out what content to create, when to publish, and where to distribute. Bridges the launch plan's "what needs to happen" with the execution skills' "here's the actual content."

**Announce at start:** "I'm using the content-strategy skill to plan the content."

## Process

**Step 1: Context gathering**
- Read existing docs: launch plan, positioning strategy, personas
- Ask the output mode question (minimal vs full)

**Step 2: Guided conversation (one area at a time)**

**Content pillars**
- Define 3-5 topic themes aligned to personas and positioning
- Each pillar: name, description, which persona(s) it serves, example topics
- Example: "Pain point education" - content showing the problem your product solves
- Push back above 5 pillars - focus beats breadth

**Content types per channel**
- Match formats to where each persona spends time
- Blog posts, social threads, videos, emails, case studies, podcasts
- Don't recommend formats the user can't produce - ask about capabilities first

**Content calendar**
- Map content to launch phases (pre-launch, launch week, post-launch)
- Frequency per channel (realistic for team size)
- Key dates and tentpole moments

**Distribution plan**
- Owned channels: blog, email list, social accounts
- Earned channels: PR, guest posts, podcast appearances, community posts
- Paid channels: ads, sponsorships, influencer partnerships
- Prioritize by budget and persona channel preferences

**Repurposing strategy**
- How one piece of content becomes many
- Example: blog post → social thread → email newsletter → video script
- Maximizes output from limited content creation capacity

**Step 3: Write output**
- Write `docs/marketing/content-strategy.md`
- **Full mode adds:** content calendar as JSON (dates, channels, status, pillar), content brief templates per pillar, distribution checklist per content type

**Step 4: Handoff**
- Suggest `globalcoder-marketing:landing-page` and `globalcoder-marketing:campaign-builder`

## Key Principles

- **3-5 pillars max** - focused beats comprehensive
- **Match capacity** - don't plan more content than the team can produce
- **Repurpose everything** - create once, distribute many times
- **Personas drive channels** - go where your buyers are, not where everyone is
